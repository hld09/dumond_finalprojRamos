This presentation covers how restaurants and other public spheres are defined by their attendees and ideal audience. This lecture addresses designers wishes to create spaces that are not only beautiful to inhabit, but also beautiful to share.

Design Trends Influenced by Social Media

Presented by: Ryan McCready - Date: May 7th, 2018 @ 12:00 PM

This lecture focuses on how art and murals are largely going viral based on shares on Instagram and notoriety gained through social media imagery and other forms of social media sharing.

Presented by: Jeremy Maas - Date: May 7th, 2018 @ 1:00 PM

This lecture really touches on how artists and creatives nowadays are creating performance art that is meant to be interactive, and how going viral can completely change the scope in which art and other creative outlets are disemminated.

The Marketability of Designing Social Media Friendly Spaces

Presented by: Nicky Kehoe - Date: May 7th, 2018 @ 3:30 PM

This presentation touches on why it is important to appeal to millennials and their needs when designing new Spaces and taking into consideration aesthetics they may enjoy. It is much more profitable to jump on the bandwagon of design trends than to disregard them altogether if making money is the priority.

How to Evoke Wonder and Profit from It

On Observation and Enchantment

Presented by: Charles Pétillon - Date: May 8th, 2018 @ 10:30 AM

An artist and photographer that creates immersive and evocative pieces for his audience, he is best known for his huge installation works spanning London and New York. Incorporating things such as motion and dance into his pieces, he is known for creating beyond just one realm or platform, skillfully combining many to create am emotional and performative artpiece.

How to Make Six Figures a Year from Instagram

Presented by: Lauren Bullen - Date: May 8, 2018 @ 12:00 PM

You probably recognize her from Instagram, but if you do not you have likely reblogged or reposted or liked a photo of her with the caption of 'goals'.
